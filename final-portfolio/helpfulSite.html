03. HELPFUL SITE

Asian American History through Art

Figma & Design Research

A website to educate people on the history of Asian Americans between 1850 and the present day through the lens of art. In comparison to writers and political activists, the presence of artists has been marginal in the discussion of ethnic and racial minorities. The goal of this website is to not only educate others on Asian-American history in the U.S., but also to support and highlight Asian/Asian American artists and their work.

This project is ongoing under the ASAM Fellows Program!

Interviews

When conducting research on how to best curate content for this site, 5 interviews were conducted with Asian American artists and educators. Interviewed artists included Adel Wu, Kathy Chung, and Sonya Han, while interviewed educators included Dr. Fariha Khan and Professor Rob Buscher, both of which are a part of Penn's Asian American Studies department.

Artists were asked the following questions:

  1. How can you see this website in comparison to the resources that are available now?
  2. What are other similar examples of websites highlighting Asian American history in this space?
  3. Tell me a little bit about your background. How connected are you to your cultural heritage? Where did you grow up? What generation are you? Where is your family from?
  4. Tell me about your art practice. What type of art do you do? When and how did you start doing art? Why do you continue to do art?
  5. How much of a role does culture play in your art? Is it the subject matter? Is it integrated into the style?
  6. How does activism play a role in your art?
  7. Who are artists that you admire?
  8. What are struggles that Asian or Asian-American artists face?
  9. Are there any particular artists you think I should highlight?
  10. Do you have any suggestions for who else I should talk to?
  11. Is there something I missed that you think is important?

Educators were asked the following questions:

  1. How can you see this website in comparison to the resources that are available now?
  2. What are other similar examples of websites highlighting Asian American history in this space?
  3. What time periods did Asian-American art become extremely expressive of their experience?
  4. Within Asian-American history, are there defined periods of time that are symbolic of something? If so, what are they?
  5. What are struggles that people who teach about Asian American Studies face?
  6. How representative do you think the arts are of Asian American experiences?
  7. Are there any particular artists or time periods you think I should highlight?
  8. Do you have any suggestions for who else I should talk to?
  9. Is there something I missed that you think is important?
